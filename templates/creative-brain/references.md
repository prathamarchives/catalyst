# references.md — template

## purpose

The user's admired people, brands, posts, visuals, and products — each with **what to learn** and **what not to copy**. A reference without the don't-copy note is how users end up sounding like their influences instead of themselves.

## what belongs here

- named references (people, brands, specific works)
- per reference: what the user actually admires about it (specific, not "it's great")
- per reference: what must NOT be copied (persona, slang, shock tactics, surface aesthetics)
- dead references: things the user used to admire and has moved past

## what does not belong here

- unattributed aesthetic direction (taste.md)
- references the agent finds on its own — only what the user supplied or confirmed

## suggested sections

```md
## live references
## dead references
```

## example entries

```md
## live references
### <name / work>
admires: long paragraph-style argument; receipts before claims
learn: structure of the argument, evidence density
do NOT copy: his persona, his aggression, his signature phrases

## dead references
### <old influence>
moved past 2026-03: was copying their cadence instead of having one
```

## update rule

Add only user-supplied or user-confirmed references. Every new entry requires both the learn line and the do-NOT-copy line — refuse to record one without the other. Move to dead rather than delete.
