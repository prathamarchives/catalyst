# voice.md — template

## purpose

How the user actually talks and writes — mechanics, not adjectives. "Witty and authentic" is useless; "opens with the contradiction, lowercase, short declaratives, jokes by understatement" is a voice.

## what belongs here

- rhythm: sentence length, punctuation habits, paragraph density
- casing, slang, profanity policy — as observed, not idealized
- how they open (first-line patterns, with quotes)
- how they argue, explain, joke
- register shifts: how voice differs by surface (posts vs replies vs long-form)
- evidence quotes for every pattern

## what does not belong here

- banned things (anti-slop.md)
- specific vocabulary lists (lexicon.md)
- aspirational voice the user wishes they had — record the real one

## suggested sections

```md
## spine
## rhythm and mechanics
## openings (with evidence)
## registers by surface
## fake-polish bans
```

## example entries

```md
## openings (with evidence)
pattern: starts from the dumb contradiction, plainly stated
evidence: "its funny that people really prompt ai 'write like me' and expect it to do it"

## rhythm and mechanics
- lowercase default in posts; normal casing in docs
- short declaratives; rarely more than two clauses
```

## update rule

Append new observed patterns with evidence as they appear. When user feedback contradicts a recorded pattern, the correction wins — update the pattern and note the date.
