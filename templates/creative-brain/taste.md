# taste.md — template

## purpose

What the user likes, notices, and returns to — always by contrast. Taste recorded without its rejections is just a mood board; the like/reject pairs are the actual signal.

## what belongs here

- quality markers: what they notice first in work they admire
- like/reject pairs ("loves X / allergic to Y")
- aesthetic directions with enough specificity to act on (palettes, textures, eras, energies)
- what they find premium vs cheap
- taste questions they ask themselves (if observed)

## what does not belong here

- named references with learn/don't-copy notes (references.md)
- decision rules for accepting/rejecting work (judgment.md)
- generic "good design principles" the user never expressed

## suggested sections

```md
## notices first
## like / reject pairs
## aesthetic direction
## premium vs cheap
```

## example entries

```md
## like / reject pairs
- loves: specific numbers and receipts in claims / rejects: adjective-stacked hype
- loves: grainy analog texture / rejects: clean glossy gradients

## premium vs cheap
restraint reads premium. one accent color, controlled. every added effect cheapens.
```

## update rule

Append pairs as they're observed. Each new like should hunt for its matching reject before being recorded alone. Re-confirm direction-level entries with the user quarterly — taste drifts.
